What Is the Average Lifespan of a Car Battery?

Most car batteries last between three to five years, depending on driving habits, weather conditions, and maintenance. If your battery is nearing the three-year mark, it’s a good idea to have it inspected regularly. Why Is Your Engine Slow to Start?

A slow or sluggish engine crank is one of the most noticeable signs of a failing battery. If your car hesitates to start, it might mean the battery is struggling to deliver the necessary power. Are Your Electronics Acting Up?

Modern vehicles are equipped with advanced electronics that rely heavily on the battery. Dim headlights, flickering dashboard lights, or erratic behavior of your radio and air conditioning system are red flags. If these issues occur frequently, your battery might be on its last legs and require immediate replacement.

Does Your Battery Case Look Damaged?

The physical condition of the battery itself can offer clues about its health. A bloated or cracked battery case indicates excessive heat exposure or internal damage. This problem can lead to leaks or a total failure of the battery. Much like addressing a case repair in other devices, it’s vital to fix such visible issues promptly.

Is Your Check Engine Light On?

The check engine light isn’t exclusive to engine troubles; it can also signal battery issues. Many cars are programmed to alert you if the battery's voltage drops below a critical level.
